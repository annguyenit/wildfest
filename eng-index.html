ABOUT

Vietnam and the United States are teaming up in Operation Game Change (OGC) - an alliance aimed at ending wildlife crime, especially rhino horn trafficking. Culminating in a November 2015 finale event called WildFest at one of Hanoi’s most beautiful cultural heritage sites: Imperial Citadel of Thang Long, OGC will work to positively influence millions of Vietnamese by raising public awareness and sparking a reduction in demand. OGC presents a unique opportunity to make a real difference on this urgent global issue. By working together, we can stop wildlife crime, bolster the rule of law, and preserve animals on the brink of extinction.

OGC was officially launched in Vietnam on March 3, 2015 with support from H.E. Cao Duc Phat, Minister, Ministry of Agriculture and Rural Development (MARD), H.E. Bui Cach Tuyen, Vice Minister, Ministry of Natural Resources and Environment (MONRE) both of whom were panelists at the launch event alongside: the U.S. Ambassador to Vietnam, Ted Osius; celebrity and wildlife activist MC Vu Anh Tuan; and Steven Galster, Executive Director of Freeland, OGC's lead implementing agency.

WildFest is hosting a Vietnamese short film competition to raise awareness about wildlife trafficking, to be held for the first time in 2015 with a focus on the illegal trade and consumption of rhino horn in Vietnam. The WildFest short film contest is open to people of all nationalities, however all submitted films should be developed specifically in relation to the consumption of rhino horn products in Vietnam. WildFest is part of the Operation Game Change (OGC) Campaign - an alliance aimed at ending wildlife crime, especially rhino horn trafficking.

WildFest has been established to encourage creative approaches to tackling the illegal wildlife trade. We would like to encourage anyone who is passionate about using film to make a difference to enter this competition. We aim to provide you with the opportunity to showcase your work to as wide an audience as possible, not only with the fantastic premiere your film will receive if selected for the WildFest Hanoi 2015 festival event, but also by utilising a variety of distribution outlets following the festival. We expect that over 5,000 people will come together in Hanoi for the WildFest event on Sunday 1 November 2015 to view the top selected short films from across the country, and that hundreds of thousands more will see the films online. This is an amazing opportunity for you to use your voice and creativity to make a real difference.

Please note the following requirements when creating your short film:

• Topic: Illegal trade and consumption of wildlife products in Vietnam, with a particular focus on rhino horn.
• Author/Producer: open - all nationalities, all ages, genders and professions.
• Production: all genres.
• Timing: the film must be produced between 01/01/2014 and 31/08/2015.
• Language: Vietnamese-speaking with English subtitles; other languages must have Vietnamese or English subtitles. Subtitles should be rendered onto each film (hard subs).
• Runtime/ Duration: Maximum 7 minutes including titles and credits. Strong preference for films between 5 and 7 minutes.
• Format: High Definition 1080p, file format: .mp4. (please see technical specifications for submission below)

Judging of The Films
a) Up to ten (10) films will be chosen as finalists (‘Official Selection’) for viewing and judging by the selected juries.

b) WildFest may, at its absolute discretion and without giving reasons, short-list a film to be included in the Official Selection.

c) WildFest jurors to judge the Finalists of the Grand and Special Prizes will be comprised of industry professionals (including actors, directors, writers and producers) and leading wildlife experts knowledgeable about this issue in Vietnam. The Press & Critics Choice Award jury will be comprised of media professionals including journalists and film critics). The members of each Jury will watch the Official Selection films and score them based on key criteria. WildFest will tally the results and determine an average score for each film. The highest score wins.

d) Key criteria for evaluation by the jurors includes: I. Strength of idea & creative concept; II. Production execution; III. The strongest message integration regarding the illegal wildlife trade and consumption of rhino horn; IV. Relevance of the film to target audience groups.

1. Do I have to shoot a scene with a rhino in my film for WildFest?
No. WildFest encourages filmmakers to creatively approach each film primarily from a consumer-­‐driven messaging angle as Vietnam is one of the highest consumers of illegal wild life products in the world.

2. What are the target audience groups of WildFest?
 As referenced in the key messaging document, WildFest has highlighted 3 target groups: (i) the consumers: the business community (social status seekers) and the government community (those targeted for gifts to gain favour); (ii) the change makers: Vietnamese youth & the next generation; and (iii) purchasers: includes family caretakers often women

3. Does a film for WildFest have to target all the three audience groups?
 Not necessarily, however we recommend targeting at least one target audience group. General messaging towards multiple audience groups would also be accepted.

4. Is it true that if my film targets the consumers group rather than the other two, it will be easier to get selected for the competing Official Selection?
No. The focus target audience is up to each film maker to decide and there will be no preference to any one group for the official selection. More important is the quality of each film in relation to how the effectively the key messages are creatively integrated.

5. My film is more than 7 minutes in length. Is it eligible for WildFest?
The maximum duration for all short film entries is 7 minutes (and ideally a minimum of 5 mins but we accept less).

6. Does a film submitted to WildFest need to be a fiction?
 No, we encourage all film genres: fiction, non-­‐fiction (documentary), animation and everything in-­‐between (crossover).

7. Can I use music and footages from other sources for my film without clearance of the copyrights?
No. All filmmakers should collect signed release forms for all works in which copyright exists. This includes literary (e.g. if your film is based on a novel, a script etc.), dramatic (e.g. performers/actors), artistic (e.g. paintings etc.), music works, sound recordings, broadcasts and published editions.

8. Are there any rhinos left in Vietnam?
 The last wild Java rhino was killed in 2010 at Nam Cat Tien National Park. Nowadays, there are only some rhinos in the zoos and in the farms of some individuals.

9. Is rhino farming a good way to protect rhinos?
 No. We don’t encourage rhino and wildlife farming. Because farming costs are very high and it would increase the consumption of wildlife products, as the consumers would think that wildlife can be farmed so consumption is okay.

10. If I have a question / enquiry about the consumption of wildlife/rhino horns or about the WildFest film contest, where can I get the answer?
For information related to the WildFest Film Contest please see www.wildfest.org. If you still have any enquiry, please send us an email to film@wildfest.org.

OUR PROFESSIONAL FILMMAKERS



NGUYEN HOANG DIEP Director

NGUYEN HOANG DIEP is a prominent face among the independent filmmakers in Vietnam. She is the writer-director-producer of Flapping in the Middle of Nowhere which premiered at Venice International Film Critics’ Week in September 2014 and won the FEDEORA Best Film Award. The film was selected for Toronto, Busan, AFI Fest,… and got 8 awards since then. Its commercial release in Vietnam in January 2015 has been a success as the first arthouse feature film distributed independently nationwide. In 2008, Diep founded VBLOCK Media, an independent studio focused on but not limited to art film and experimental projects. Diep and VBLOCK Media have successfully produced some feature film projects, among them Bi, don’t be Afraid! which premiered at Cannes Semaine de la Critique 2010. The film won a record number of awards at international film festivals. Diep lives in Hanoi. She is married and a mother to two children. She is working on the script of her second feature film, while she acts as a co-producer for some other film projects.

BAO NGUYEN Director

BAO NGUYEN is a Vietnamese American filmmaker whose past work has been seen in the New York Times, HBO, Vice, ARTE, PBS, among many others. He has directed, produced, and shot a number of short films, which have played internationally in numerous festivals and museums including MoMA PS1 and the Smithsonian. His graduate thesis film, Julian, won a CINE Golden Eagle Award, the Best Student Documentary Short at the Palm Springs ShortsFest, the Special Jury Prize at DOCNYC, and was nominated for an IDA Award. He was the Co-Producer and Cinematographer of Where Heaven Meets Hell which played the largest documentary festival in the world, IDFA, in 2012 and premiered on American television in summer 2013. He produced and DPed Nuoc 2030, a feature narrative set in near future Vietnam which opened the Panorama section of the 2014 Berlin International Film Festival and was a recipient of the TFI Sloan Filmmaker Award. His feature directorial debut, Live From New York! opened the 2015 Tribeca Film Festival, one of the most prestigious film festivals in America and will play theatrically across the US this summer. He is a 2011 PBS/WGBH Producers Workshop Fellow and an alumnus of the 2012 and 2014 Berlinale Talent Campus. He earned his BA in Politics/International Relations at NYU and his MFA in Social Documentary Film at the School of Visual Arts in New York City.

NGUYEN QUANG DUNG Director

NGUYEN QUANG DUNG graduated from the first directing class of the Film School in Ho Chi Minh City. He directed his first theatrical feature in 2005, Hon Truong Ba, Da Hang Thit (Truong Ba's soul in the Butcher's body). After this promising debut, Nguyen Quang Dung made four big hits at the box-office: Nu Hon Than Chet (A Kiss of Death), Giai Cuu Than Chet (Rescue The Death) and Nhung Nu Hon Ruc Ro (Bright Kisses) - the first Vietnamese commercial musical and My Nhan Ke (The Lady Assassin) - a martial art movie. Not only success in film, Nguyen Quang Dung is also popular as music show producer, television game-show director, Vietnam's idol judge, music composer and magazine columnist. His latest feature called Sieu Nhan X (Superman X) was among the big titles of the New Year’s release Season 2015.
